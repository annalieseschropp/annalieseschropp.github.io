About BitBakery

Trusted outsourced software development

BitBakery is a development company focused on providing secure outsourced solutions unique to any company large or small. The team designs and builds custom applications that are engaging and user-friendly while aligning with client's wants and needs to bring forth successful products. Whether it's for the web, mobile, or something else, everyone is able to work together and create the desired vision.

Founded in 2014, the company started off with just a handful of developers and designers but has grown to over 10 employees!

My Role at BitBakery

Throughout my time with BitBakery, I was given the opportunity to work on a couple different projects. This included following the complete development lifecycle for one client as well as delving into researching a brand new project for another.

My role as an Application Software Developer at BitBakery exposed me to completely new experiences and technologies. As a coop student I am often unable to see projects from start to finish, however this position allowed me to experience everything from sprint 1 to final testing. From researching external providers to development to QA, I was exposed to all aspects of the project. I was given several opportunities to present research findings, provide my opinions, and demonstrate my work to receive feedback.

I primarily worked on the development of a new product. This entailed using React Typescript with a Lambda Serverless backend and more to create a bridge between users and an external system to streamline their workflow. Since this was a brand new product and prior to this role I had little experience using this tech stack, I developed my skills as I became more familiar with the tools. Due to client confiendtiality, I am unable to explain in detail the specifics of the project.

Following that, I began another project. This entailed researching viable external providers to help narrow down the scope, creating and presenting a proof of concept, and general project structure setup. In my experience coop students are not typically given tasks related to the earliest stages of projects, so this was a very unique role for me.

BitBakery Work Term Goals

Inquiry & Analysis

Be able to present meaningful information and questions regarding situations, technology and systems, gather and evaluate relevant evidence, and articulate how this justifies the decision or conclusion and how it contributes to the project.

  • When researching, keep well organized notes that are relevant to the the question being asked
  • Provide a variety of options and lines of thinking to present all relevant options available
  • Present data and information in a manageable way but also leaves room for an open discussion regarding the topic

Technological Literacy

Learn new technologies and languages with a focus on back-end development.

  • Gain an understanding of Amazon Web Services including Lambdas, DynamoDB, Cognito, and more
  • Gain an understanding of Amazon Web Services including Lambdas, DynamoDB, Cognito, and more
  • Apply that understanding to build a successful product or script

Oral Communication

Articulate technologies, ideas, and concepts to a wide variety of skill levels and backgrounds to provide context and understanding to the work I am doing.

  • Within the development team, provide continual updates for tickets during meetings, standup, and also within slack
  • For clients or non-technical members, minimize the amount of technical jargon, abbreviations, etc. to ensure a fuller understanding of what has been accomplished
  • If questions arise, walk through the concepts carefully and clearly explain
  • Ensure no explanation is overally lengthy so as to not waste anyone's time

Progress of my Goals and Feedback

I personally thrive on feedback, which is why one of my favourite experiences I took from working at BitBakery was the one on one meetings with my supervisor Daniel. During these conversations, we were able to speak regarding the progress of the projects and my work itself. I found that within these meetings my opinion and ideas were taken into serious consideration.

During my final evalutation, I recieved feedback regarding confidence in group discussions. I plan on taking this feedback and applying it towards future roles and academic terms in order to gain more confidence in my abilities.

How BitBakery Relates to my Academics

I am currently studying computer science and working as a developer helped give me insight into the inner workings of what I was studying academically and allowing me to apply my knowledge as well as develop new skills. Through BitBakery, I not only was given the opportunity to gain knowledge of new tools and languages, but I also learned how the research and early stages of a project can be accomplished.

Applying Technical Knowledge
Prior to this coop experience, I had completed the Cloud Computing course. It provided me a general knowledge base for working with cloud databases and functions at a small scale. Through this role I was able to expand on that experience and apply it on a much larger scale. Without the original exposure to this, I would have felt more overwhelmed, however the University of Guelph prepared me to understand the very basics of cloud technologies.

Research
Researching is a fundamental skill that anyone in a developer role must have. Throughout my education, I have honed this skill and in this role I was able to apply that knowledge. From helping decide the best authentication provider to researching various external APIs, being able to read documentation and understand it is a necessary strength to have both professionally and academically.

Communication
It's extremely important to communicate clearly both verbally and in writing. Through my experiences in group projects, and in other positions, good communication prevents confusion and fosters understanding. This is why there is emphasis within academic settings on clear communication. Especially in a virtual setting, communication is key in having the project run smoothly and minimize wasted time. The University of Guelph prepared me for this while BitBakery allowed me to put it in practice.

My Focus and Main Takeaways
from BitBakery

The main takeaway I had working for BitBakery was the work. I found that this position was exactly what I could see myself doing post graduation. Not only did I have the opportunity to work on multiple projects but I continued to be exposed to new technologies. I felt as though the contributions I made to the projects actually had an impact and being able to see my work front and center fulfills me greatly.

Not only was the work fulfilling but the people behind the company provide such a wonderful atmosphere. Being a small company creates a tightly knitted community that helps foster teamwork. I could reach out to anyone for help and similarly could provide assistance when needed. I felt welcomed and a part of the team during my coop with BitBakery.

Final Thoughts

Working for BitBakery has been a wonderful experience. I learned so much during my time with them that I will take with me into future jobs such as teamwork, communication, and creative thinking. Not only did this co-op opportunity provide me with help towards my desired goals, but they also gave me insight into what it would be like working as a developer.

I hope to return to BitBakery post graduation because of the community and the extremely unique and interesting projects.
